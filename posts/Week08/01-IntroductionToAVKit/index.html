Overview

Suggested Reading:
AVKit (Apple Documentation)
Delivering Intuitive Media Playback with AVKit (WWDC 2019)

AVKit is a framework for displaying media in your app. It provides user controls, chapter navigation, subtitles and closed captioning, and picture in picture on the iPad. If you’ve ever watched video on your iOS device, chances are you used AVKit.

Let’s spend a few minutes talking about the more advanced topics in AVKit, since starting a playback of video is fairly straightforward, especially thanks to iOS 14's new VideoPlayer SwiftUI view, which we'll take a look at in the example.

Navigation Markers

Navigations makers in AVKit are represented by AVNavigationMarkersGroup objects, and are lists of markers for navigating playback. Typically this is in the form of a chapter list, but can represent any time based event in the audiovisual stream. The AVTimedMetadataGroups class contains time and metadata information, and can be assigned to the AVPlayerItem's navigationMarkerGroups property.

Picture in Picture Mode

Picture in Picture is available on iPad and iPhone (which got this feature in iOS 14), and allows you to present video playback in a floating resizable window. Although there is a standalone class called AVPictureInPictureController (which requires additional checks and control on your part), you don't need to use it - as long as you use AVPlayerViewController, and the device you're on supports Picture in Picture, you will automatically get controls and built in behavior for PiP.

AirPlay Support

In addition to natively supporting Picture in Picture, AVPlayerViewController also natively supports AirPlay which allows you to play video content to your Apple TV and stream audio content to third-party Airplay compatible speakers and receivers. You do, however, need to set some project and audio session configurations - you can find more info here

Generating Thumbnails

Finally, even though it isn’t part of AVKit, you can use the AVAssetImageGenerator class to generate thumbnails from a video. Videos can be on your device or remote, and if they are remote, you can asynchronously generate them for use in your app. See the example below for more details.
